The Proof, Made Concrete

⏳ Living Without a Crystal Ball

🎿

Ski Rental's 2x Bound

Rent until cumulative rent cost equals the buy price, then buy — this deterministic strategy guarantees a competitive ratio no worse than 2, and no deterministic algorithm can do better in the worst case. It's a genuinely tight, provable bound.

🎲

Randomization Helps

A randomized ski-rental strategy — buying at a randomly chosen day drawn from a specific distribution — achieves an expected competitive ratio of e/(e-1) ≈ 1.58, provably better than any deterministic algorithm can guarantee against a worst-case adversary.

📅

Real-World Online Decisions

Cloud auto-scaling (rent more servers vs buy reserved capacity), cache eviction (LRU is itself an online algorithm), and ad auction bidding all face this exact "decide now, no take-backs" structure — competitive ratio is how their worst-case performance is actually measured.

🔒

Adversarial Arrival Order

Competitive ratio assumes a worst-case adversary who knows your algorithm and constructs the input specifically to hurt it. This is deliberately pessimistic — but it's the only way to get a provable, unconditional performance guarantee that holds no matter what happens.
